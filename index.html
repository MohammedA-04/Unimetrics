What is: Unimetrics?


We are creating a Universal Metric Converter for Students called UniMetrics. This website will made possible by HTML and Replit where we'll aim to create a unique metric converter for Academics and <18 Students One of strengthens is that we have unique selling point whereby our website will teach students how to convert metric aswell as calculate the answer for them



Why Unimetric?


Our motivation for developing this app is to create an easy to use, efficient metric convertor to allow students in all levels of education to have quick access to all types of conversions. We believe having a reliable effective app for students to rely on will help increase the productivity of students as they will no longer have to scour the internet only to find a add-ridden, confusing metric convertor and can focus entirely on their work.


Our inspiration of our project came from Calculator.net and Metric-conversion.org which are two renown metric converters within Metric Conversion Market. Upon inspected inspect of these metric conversion websites, the design of these pages was overloaded with information and not UI friendly with students (<18) who are usually the most common demographic who access these websites.


History of metrics

The metric system is an international system used to measure weights, temperature, length, area, volume, speed, time, angle, engery and much more. The Frenchman Gabriel Mounton is accreddited as the founding father of the metric system. In 1670 Mouton proposed the concept of a decimal system of measurement that which would be later refined by other French Scientists. The word "Metric" was inspired from the Greek word "Metron or measure". Which is where the metric called meter got it's name from.
